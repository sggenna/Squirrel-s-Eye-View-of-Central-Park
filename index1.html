Flee rate vs annual human visits by park zone (Conservancy entrance data, 2009)

Key finding: Zones receiving 4M+ annual visits (Wollman Rink, SW Corner/Merchants Gate, Great Lawn) show flee rates 2.5 to 3× higher than quieter northern zones. The pattern suggests sustained human density creates two distinct squirrel populations: a habituated majority (indifferent) and a stress-conditioned minority that flees immediately. Both the 2018 Census and 2009 Conservancy survey point to the same southern concentration of activity as the driver.
